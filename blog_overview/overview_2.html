1. Optimal Truncation Position


Explanation

After continuously selecting, we use differentiable techniques to find the best truncation position. This is a very natural idea, but previous works haven't done this because they couldn't solve this issue:

Since lots of singular values in the low-rank matrix are of similar size, and during SVD backpropagation, there's an operator: $\frac{1}{\sigma_i - \sigma_j}$, one divided by the difference of singular i and singular j, which can lead to gradient explosion.

We then use the Taylor Expansion to change the subtraction into a division form.

This is not a common method for handling gradient explosion. Besides our work, we found only one paper1, which also addresses the backpropagation issue of SVD, and it focuses on lower-dimensional symmetric matrices and CV tasks, whereas we are dealing with higher-dimensional general matrices and applying it on large models. If you're interested in the impact of the Taylor Expansion on gradients, you can refer to that paper.

[1] Robust Differentiable SVD, TPAMI 2021
